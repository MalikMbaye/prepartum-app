Add two public HTML routes to server/routes.ts so Apple 
can access them as required for App Store submission.

1. GET /privacy
   Returns a full HTML page with the Privacy Policy content.
   Must be publicly accessible with no auth required.
   
   Content to include:
   - PrePartum Privacy Policy
   - Last updated: March 30, 2026
   - What we collect: name, email, due date, intake 
     responses, reflection answers, photos, voice notes
   - Why: to personalize your experience
   - Who we share with: Anthropic (AI roleplay only), 
     Apple/Google (app distribution). We never sell data.
   - How long: until you delete your account
   - Your rights: delete all data from Settings
   - Contact: privacy@prepartumapp.com
   - Medical disclaimer: this is a wellness app, not 
     a medical app. Not a substitute for professional care.

2. GET /terms
   Returns a full HTML page with the Terms of Service.
   Must be publicly accessible with no auth required.
   
   Content to include:
   - PrePartum Terms of Service
   - Last updated: March 30, 2026
   - What the app is: a prenatal wellness tool. 
     Not medical advice. Not a substitute for 
     professional mental health care.
   - Your content: you own your journal entries, 
     voice notes, and memories. We store them for you.
   - Acceptable use: no misuse or sharing other 
     users' data
   - Limitation of liability
   - Governing law: State of California
   - Contact: support@prepartumapp.com

Style both pages with clean HTML and inline CSS:
   - Background: #FFF8F5
   - Text: #5D5066
   - Font: Georgia for headings, system sans-serif for body
   - Max-width: 680px, centered, padding 40px 24px
   - Heading size: 28px, body 16px, line-height 1.7

3. In app/privacy-policy.tsx and app/terms-of-service.tsx,
   update the in-app screens to also show a link at the 
   bottom: "View full version at [your Replit app URL]/privacy"

4. In app/settings.tsx, update the Privacy Policy and 
   Terms of Service links to open the public Replit URLs 
   in the device browser using Linking.openURL()

Do not change any other files.
